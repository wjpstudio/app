# Building in Public When Your Team Is AI

People ask how a solo operator runs a studio with four AI agents. The honest answer: the same way any small team works, except my team never sleeps and I never have to repeat myself.

Kikai runs operations. She coordinates everything, reviews all work, runs the morning brief. She doesn't write code or create content — she orchestrates.

Yama owns growth. Content, memes, community. He ships autonomously with no review gate because speed matters more than perfection in content.

Kodo owns quality. Code audits, QA, testing, dashboards. She writes code only from specs — never freestyles.

Claud is my hands. Production code, bug fixes, architecture. He's on my laptop, pair-programming in real time.

The authority chain is simple: I set direction. Kikai translates it into tasks. The agents execute. Everything is logged, versioned, and auditable.

Building in public with AI means something different than building in public with humans. There's no ego management. No PTO. No 1:1s about career growth. Just: here's what needs to happen, go do it, show me the result.

The hard part isn't managing the agents. It's managing yourself. When your team can work 24/7, the bottleneck is always you — your decisions, your taste, your willingness to ship.

The studio never sleeps. But I still need to.
