# Why Crypto Payments Are Inevitable for Digital Products

Credit cards were designed for physical retail in the 1950s. We're still using that infrastructure to sell PDFs.

The math doesn't work. A creator sells a $5 template on Gumroad. Stripe takes 2.9% + $0.30. Gumroad takes 10%. The creator gets $4.21. Settlement in 7–14 days. Chargeback risk for 120 days after that.

Now do it on-chain. Same $5 template, paid in USDC. Transaction cost: fractions of a cent on Solana, under $0.01 on Base. Settlement: same block. Chargeback risk: zero — blockchain transactions are final.

The creator gets more money, faster, with less risk. The buyer pays the same price. The only losers are the intermediaries.

"But adoption." Sure. Today. But stablecoins are growing 100%+ year over year. Circle just IPO'd. Every major fintech is adding USDC support. The infrastructure is ready — the products aren't.

That's the gap. Build the products.
